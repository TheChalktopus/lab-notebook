Fiber EOM Testing

Sideband

\[16 \text{dBm} = 10\log_{10}{\left(\frac{(V_\text{pp}/(2\sqrt{2}))^2}{50}\right)} + 30\] \[V_\text{pp} \approx 4 \text{V}\] Using, \(\beta = V_\text{pp}/(2 V_\pi)\), we find \(\Beta \approx 0.42\), which conveniently gives a ratio peaks of \[\frac{J_1(\beta)^2}{J_0(\beta)^2 + 2J_1(\beta)^2} = 0.042\]

On the scope we actually, find a ratio of \(0.026\).

Making the reasonable assumption that we have around 2 dBm of loss, then we can repeat this calculation for 14 dBm and we expect it to be closer to what we actually measure. We find \(\beta = 0.34\), which gives a ratio \(0.028\).

Power through EOM

We're sending 14 mW to EOM, getting 80 \(\muW\) out.

Looking at the FP peaks, they do seem to vary in peak height over time quite a bit. However, due to how little power we're getting through the EOM, I'm not sure if that is due to power fluctuations in the laser itself.
